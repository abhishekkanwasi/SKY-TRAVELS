Hampta Pass Trek Overview

Embarking on a trek in India is a significant achievement in itself, and trekking in Himachal Pradesh elevates that experience to a whole new level. Among the various trekking destinations in Himachal, the Hampta Pass Trek stands out as a dramatic adventure nestled at an altitude of 14,035 feet.

Starting from the scenic town of Manali, the Hampta Pass Trek provides ample opportunity to soak in the beauty of this popular destination. Known for its stunning landscapes, this trek is considered one of the most picturesque in the Himachal region.

Spanning five thrilling days, the trek begins and ends in Manali, offering an immersive journey through the heart of the Himalayas in Himachal Pradesh. Along the way, trekkers are treated to a stunning array of vibrant landscapes, lush greenery, dense forests of pine and oak, and colorful, blooming flora that stretch far beyond imagination.

The entire journey feels like a walk through a fantastical garden, with adventure waiting at every step. The Hampta Pass Trek is renowned for its exhilarating experience, making it a perfect choice for those who seek the ultimate adventure. This trek is ideal for thrill-seekers and adventure lovers alike, making the Hampta Pass trek package a must-pick for anyone looking to experience the wild beauty of the Himalayas.

Best Time to Visit Hampta Pass Trek

If you're a snow enthusiast, the best months to undertake the Hampta Pass trek are June and July. During this period, the biting cold can plunge temperatures to as low as -15 degrees Celsius, offering a thrilling chill and the chance to experience snowfall. The abundant snow is a major attraction, drawing many tourists in these months.

However, if you prefer milder weather and want to witness lush greenery, abundant flora and fauna, and the sound of rushing streams and rivers, plan your trek from mid-July to the first week of October. During this time, the landscape is vibrant with life, and you can enjoy the natural beauty without the extreme cold. October typically brings fresh snowfall, making the trek inaccessible until the following June.

In summer, shepherds often bring their sheep to graze on the unique high-altitude grasslands of Hampta Pass. The trek through the forest offers rich experiences with diverse flora and fauna, including oak, fir, maple, birch, and pine trees, interspersed with patches of lingering snow. The scenery includes clear blue skies, flowing rivers, cascading waterfalls, lush green meadows dotted with colorful wildflowers, and the snow-covered peaks of the Pir Panjal range in the backdrop.

This region is renowned for its stunning landscapes and offers numerous trails for trekking and other adventurous activities, making it a perfect destination for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike.

Trek Difficulty

The Hampta Pass trek varies in difficulty depending on the season and weather conditions. It involves navigating diverse terrain, including steep ascents and descents, and requires a good level of physical fitness and endurance. Trekkers should be prepared for changing weather patterns and altitude effects. With proper preparation, including acclimatization and suitable gear, most moderately fit individuals can complete the trek comfortably. It's advisable to seek guidance from local experts and carry essential trekking equipment for a safe and enjoyable journey.

How to Reach Hampta Pass Trek

The Hampta Pass trek starts from the popular town of Manali, which is well-connected by road. Although Kullu, situated around 60 kilometers from Manali, has an airport, flight services are limited. The most convenient way to reach Manali is by overnight bus from Chandigarh or Delhi. The journey from Delhi to Manali usually takes about 12 to 14 hours, while the travel time from Chandigarh to Manali is approximately 8 to 10 hours.

Hampta Pass Trek: Itinerary

Day 1: Manali to Jobra and Trek to Chikka (10,100 ft)
  • Drive: 30 km, Approx. 2.5 hours
  • Trek: 2-3 hours

After breakfast, embark on a scenic drive to Jobra, the starting point of your trek. The trek to Chikka takes about 2-3 hours and includes traversing a small forest patch and crossing two wooden bridges before reaching the campsite. Chikka is known for its large rock boulders, a beautiful waterfall, and offers a unique riverside camping experience.

Accommodation: Tents (Triple sharing basis)

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, and Dinner

Day 2: Chikka to Balu ka Ghera (11,900 ft)
  • Trek: 7 km, Approx. 5 hours

Start the day with breakfast before heading out on the trek to Balu ka Ghera. The trek begins along the right bank of the Hamta River and involves navigating a few rock boulders. You'll cross a water stream and continue trekking for about 5 hours to reach Balu ka Ghera. Marvel at the breathtaking views of the snow-capped Dhauladhar ranges, part of the outer Himalayan chain, as you pass through flower-filled meadows and valleys. Enjoy a leisurely evening at the campsite.

Accommodation: Tents at Balu ka Ghera

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, and Dinner

Day 3: Balu ka Ghera to Sheagoru (12,900 ft) via Hampta Pass (14,100 ft)
  • Trek: 7 km, Approx. 8 hours

Begin the day early with breakfast before embarking on a trek that showcases the dynamic landscapes of the Himalayas. This trail is perfect for beginners, offering gradual slopes and chances to interact with local shepherds. Trek through the enchanting Hampta Valley and the stunning Lahaul Valley, encountering diverse terrain. The descent to the campsite is steep and challenging, but the final kilometer is relatively flat. Sheagoru, meaning "Cold Street" in the local language, is known for its chilly winds and is considered the most beautiful campsite on the Hampta Pass trek.

Accommodation: Tents at Sheagoru

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, and Dinner

Day 4: Sheagoru to Chatru and Drive to Chandratal | Return to Chatru
  • Trek: 3 hours

After breakfast, begin the descent to Chatru, which takes about 3 hours. As you trek through the Hampta Valley, you'll be greeted by the magnificent Pir Panjal ranges. Upon arriving in Chatru, take some time to relax and appreciate the scenic beauty. Afterward, enjoy a drive through the majestic trails and passes leading to Chandratal, situated at 14,000 feet. After exploring Chandratal, return to Chatru for an overnight stay.

Accommodation: Tents at Chatru

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, and Dinner

Day 5: Jeep Ride from Chatru to Manali

Following breakfast, embark on a jeep ride back to Manali via the Atal Tunnel, arriving around 1 PM. When booking return bus tickets, consider scheduling a Volvo bus after 7 PM to allow for potential traffic delays on the journey to Manali.

Accommodation: None

Meals: Breakfast Only

Hampta Pass Trek FAQ

  1. Is the Hampta Pass Trek Suitable for Beginners?

    2. Yes, the Hampta Pass Trek is highly suitable for beginners. With an altitude of 14,035 feet, it offers a manageable challenge for those new to trekking, making it an ideal choice for novice adventurers.

  2. What Are the Fitness Requirements for the Hampta Pass Trek?

    3. The Hampta Pass Trek is graded as easy to moderate, making it a good option for first-timers who are physically and mentally fit. It is recommended to start preparing two weeks prior to the trek by walking for 30 minutes daily and gradually increasing the intensity to build adequate fitness levels.

  3. What Is the Distance of the Hampta Pass Trek?

    4. The Hampta Pass Trek covers a distance of 35 kilometers.

  4. What Is the Best Time to Trek the Hampta Pass?

    5. The best time to undertake the Hampta Pass Trek is from mid-June to mid-October. During these months, the weather is pleasant, making it an ideal time for trekking.

  5. Which Mobile Networks Work on the Hampta Pass Trek?

    6. The Hampta Pass Trek is located at an altitude of 14,035 feet, where mobile network coverage is limited. However, you may get occasional connectivity with Airtel and Idea networks at certain points along the trek.

  6. Is There a Risk of Wild Animals or Avalanches on the Hampta Pass Trek?

    7. The risk of avalanches on the Hampta Pass Trek is very low, but there is a slight risk of encountering wild animals. It is advisable to stay with your trekking group and not venture off alone for safety reasons.

  7. Should I Bring High-Altitude Medicines for the Hampta Pass Trek?

    8. While high-altitude medicines are not generally necessary, some first-time trekkers may experience symptoms like vomiting, drowsiness, or difficulty breathing due to the altitude. It is a good idea to bring medication such as Diamox for altitude sickness, just in case.

  8. Is Alcohol or Smoking Allowed on the Hampta Pass Trek?

    9. No, alcohol and smoking are not recommended on the Hampta Pass Trek. Consuming alcohol at high altitudes can lead to severe health issues, and smoking reduces oxygen intake, which is essential for trekking. It is important to prioritize your health and avoid these substances.

  9. What Is the Age Limit for the Hampta Pass Trek?

    10. The minimum age requirement for the Hampta Pass Trek is between 9 to 10 years.
