Chopta Tungnath Trek Overview

The Chopta Tungnath Trek is a blend of natural beauty, spirituality, and adventure. Here’s a detailed overview of what to expect:

The trek begins from the serene village of Chopta, often referred to as the “Mini Switzerland of India” due to its lush green meadows and stunning mountain views. From Chopta, the trail ascends gently through dense forests and meadows, leading to the sacred Tungnath Temple. This temple is one of the Panch Kedar and holds significant religious importance.

After paying respects at the temple, trekkers can continue to Chandrashila peak. The ascent to Chandrashila is steeper, but the reward at the top is a panoramic view that encompasses several of the highest peaks in the Indian Himalayas. On clear days, you can see Nanda Devi, Trishul, Kedar Peak, Bandarpunch, and Chaukhamba.

The entire trek can be completed in two to three days, making it a perfect short adventure. The trail is well-maintained and marked, with stone-paved paths and steps leading up to the temple and summit. The journey is enriched with beautiful rhododendron forests that bloom in the spring, adding vibrant colors to the landscape.

Best Time to Visit Chopta Tungnath Trek

Choosing the right time for the Chopta Tungnath Trek can significantly enhance your experience. Here’s a breakdown of the best seasons:

Summer (April to June): During summer, the weather is pleasant and the skies are clear. This is the most popular time for the trek, as the temperatures are mild, ranging from 10°C to 25°C. The meadows are lush and green, and the views of the Himalayas are unobstructed.

Autumn (September to November): Autumn is another great time for the trek, with post-monsoon greenery and clear skies. The temperatures range from 5°C to 20°C, and the trails are less crowded. This season offers crisp air and vibrant landscapes, making it ideal for photography and bird watching.

Winter (December to March): For those seeking a winter adventure, the trek can be done during this time, though it becomes more challenging. The region is covered in snow, and temperatures can drop below freezing. While the temple and summit may be inaccessible due to heavy snow, the snow-covered trails and landscapes provide a unique and serene experience.

Trek Difficulty

Understanding the difficulty level of the Chopta Tungnath Trek helps in preparing appropriately:

Level: The trek is categorized as easy to moderate. It involves gradual ascents and well-defined paths, making it suitable for beginners. Experienced trekkers will find it a pleasant and relatively straightforward hike.

Elevation: The maximum altitude reached is 4,000 meters at Chandrashila summit. The altitude gain is manageable, reducing the risk of altitude sickness.

Duration: Typically, the trek is completed in 2-3 days. The first day involves reaching Chopta, the second day is spent trekking to Tungnath and Chandrashila, and the third day is for returning.

Things to Know and Carry

Proper preparation ensures a safe and enjoyable trekking experience. Here’s what you need to know and carry:

Essential Gear:
  • Trekking Shoes: Comfortable, waterproof trekking shoes with good grip are essential.
  • Clothing: Layered clothing is recommended. Carry warm clothes, including thermal wear, a fleece jacket, a down jacket, and waterproof pants.
  • Rain Gear: A raincoat or poncho is necessary, especially during the monsoon season.
  • Accessories: Gloves, hats, sunglasses, and a sun cap are important for protection against cold and sun.
Backpack Essentials:
  • Water Bottles: Carry at least 2 liters of water. Hydration is crucial at high altitudes.
  • Snacks: Energy bars, dry fruits, and chocolates for quick energy boosts.
  • First-aid Kit: Include bandages, antiseptic cream, painkillers, and personal medications.
  • Trekking Pole: A trekking pole can help with balance and reduce strain on your knees.
  • Personal Hygiene Items: Wet wipes, hand sanitizer, and biodegradable toiletries.
Permits and Documentation:
  • No special permits are required for the Chopta Tungnath Trek, but it’s advisable to carry a valid ID proof.
  • Inform your family or friends about your trekking plans and expected return date.

How to Reach Chopta Tungnath Trek

Reaching the starting point of the trek, Chopta, involves multiple modes of transportation. Here’s a detailed guide on how to get there:

By Air:

The nearest airport is Jolly Grant Airport in Dehradun, which is approximately 221 km from Chopta. From the airport, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to reach Chopta.

By Train:

The nearest railway station is Haridwar Railway Station, around 200 km from Chopta. Haridwar is well-connected with major cities like Delhi, Mumbai, and Kolkata. From Haridwar, you can hire a taxi or take a bus to Chopta.

By Road:

From Rishikesh, Chopta is approximately 162 km and can be reached by a 7-8 hour drive. Buses and taxis are available from Rishikesh to Chopta. The road journey offers scenic views of the Garhwal Himalayas, crossing towns like Devprayag and Rudraprayag.

Chopta Tungnath Trek: Itinerary

Day 1: Rishikesh to Chopta
  • Distance: 162 km
  • Duration: 7-8 hours drive

Start early from Rishikesh to make the most of the day. The drive takes you through scenic routes with views of the Ganges River, passing through Devprayag, where the Alaknanda and Bhagirathi rivers meet to form the Ganges. Continue through Rudraprayag, another confluence point, before reaching Chopta. Upon arrival, check into a guesthouse or camp, and enjoy the serene surroundings of Chopta. Acclimatize to the altitude and explore the local area.

Accommodation: Guesthouse or Camp

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, and Dinner

Day 2: Chopta to Tungnath and Chandrashila Summit
  • Distance: 5 km (Trek)
  • Duration: 3-4 hours to Tungnath, 1 hour to Chandrashila

Start early to maximize your time at the summit. The trek begins with a gradual ascent through dense forests of pine and rhododendron. The well-paved trail leads you to Tungnath Temple, the highest Shiva temple in the world. Spend some time exploring the temple and its surroundings.

From Tungnath, the trail to Chandrashila becomes steeper. The final ascent is challenging but rewarding, with panoramic views from the summit. On clear days, the view encompasses several major Himalayan peaks.

After soaking in the views and taking photographs, descend back to Tungnath and then to Chopta. Return to your accommodation for the night.

Accommodation: Guesthouse or Camp

Meals: Breakfast, Lunch, and Dinner

Day 3: Chopta to Rishikesh
  • Distance: 162 km
  • Duration: 7-8 hours drive

After breakfast, start your journey back to Rishikesh. The drive offers a chance to reflect on your trekking experience and enjoy the scenic beauty of the Garhwal Himalayas one last time. Upon arrival in Rishikesh, you can explore the town, visit the ashrams, or relax by the Ganges.

Accommodation: None

Meals: Breakfast Only

Chopta Tungnath Trek FAQ

  1. What is the weather like during different seasons on the Chopta Tungnath trek?

    2. Summer (April to June): Enjoy pleasant temperatures ranging from 10°C to 25°C with clear skies and lush green meadows.

  2. Is it safe to trek to Chopta Tungnath alone?

    3. Yes, trekking to Chopta Tungnath alone is generally safe. It's recommended to inform someone about your trekking plans, carry essential gear including a first-aid kit, and trek during daylight hours.

  3. Can I bring children or elderly family members on the Chopta Tungnath trek?

    4. The trek is suitable for children and elderly family members who are in good health and have moderate fitness levels. It's advisable to trek at a comfortable pace, take breaks as needed, and ensure everyone is properly acclimatized to the altitude.

  4. What facilities are available in Chopta for essentials like ATMs and shops?

    5. Chopta has limited facilities. There are a few shops that may offer basic essentials, but it's advisable to carry sufficient cash as ATMs are not readily available. It's also recommended to purchase all necessary trekking supplies before arriving in Chopta.

  5. What are the transport options to reach Chopta from Rishikesh?

    6. By road: It's a 7-8 hour drive covering approximately 162 km from Rishikesh to Chopta. Buses and taxis are available, offering scenic views of the Garhwal Himalayas along the route.

  6. Are there any medical facilities along the Chopta Tungnath trek route?

    7. Basic medical facilities are available in Chopta. It's advisable to carry a first-aid kit with essentials like bandages, antiseptic cream, painkillers, and any personal medications you may need. In case of emergencies, there are medical services available, but they may be limited.

  7. What is the accommodation like during the Chopta Tungnath trek?

    8. Accommodation options in Chopta range from guesthouses to campsites. Guesthouses provide basic amenities such as beds, hot water, and meals. Camping is popular, offering tents in scenic locations amidst nature. It's recommended to book accommodations in advance, especially during peak seasons, and carry essentials like sleeping bags and toiletries.

  8. Are there any specific cultural or religious practices to be aware of during the trek?

    9. The Chopta Tungnath trek passes through areas of significant religious and cultural importance. It's important to respect local customs and traditions, especially around Tungnath Temple, one of the Panch Kedar. Dress modestly, avoid loud behavior near religious sites, and seek permission before photographing locals or religious ceremonies. Engaging respectfully with the local Garhwali culture enriches the trekking experience.
