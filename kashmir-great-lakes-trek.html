Kashmir Great Lakes Trek Yatra Overview

Kashmir Great Lakes Trek is a journey that stands out for its unique charm, capturing the imagination with its very name. Nestled in the heart of the Himalayas, Kashmir is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure enthusiasts alike. This trek offers an unparalleled experience that leaves an indelible mark on those who undertake it. The pristine lakes and snow-capped mountains exude a tranquillity and majesty that are truly awe-inspiring. It's an opportunity to immerse yourself in the natural splendor of Kashmir and create lasting memories.

Located in what is often called "paradise on earth," this trek in Kashmir spans 7 to 9 days and is considered moderately challenging. With elevations reaching up to 13,000 feet and covering a distance of approximately 63 kilometers, the trek offers a profound journey of self-discovery on physical, mental, and emotional levels. The challenge of pushing your body to its limits and overcoming any doubts you may have about completing the trek brings a sense of accomplishment that is unmatched. With proper preparation, this trek can be a joyous and memorable experience.

Best Time to Visit Kashmir Great Lakes Trek

To ensure a successful and enjoyable Kashmir Great Lakes trek, it's essential to time your journey wisely. The optimal period for this adventure is during the monsoon season, particularly in July, August, and September. During these months, you can expect pleasant weather and clear skies, which provide the best conditions to fully appreciate the breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and lakes. However, it's crucial to check the weather forecast before you set off to ensure a safe and memorable experience

How to Reach Kashmir Great Lakes Trek Trek

The Kashmir Great Lakes trek begins in Shitkadi Village, located just beyond Sonamarg and about a 3-hour drive from Srinagar.

Kashmir Great Lakes Trek Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival at Shitkadi Base Camp
  • Your journey begins at Shitkadi Village, just beyond Sonamarg.
  • Travel: Drive from Srinagar to Sonamarg (approx. 3 hours).
  • Activities: Soak in the beauty of Kashmir Valley.

The drive from Srinagar to Sonamarg offers a glimpse of the adventure ahead, with magnificent landscapes en route to Shitkadi.

Day 2: Trek from Shitkadi to Nichnai
  • Activities: 3-hour ascent, 1-hour descent, and gradual climb to Nichnai.

This day sets the stage for the natural wonders you’ll experience on your journey through the Kashmir Great Lakes.

Day 3: Nichnai to Vishnusar Lake
  • Distance: 12 km
  • Duration: Approximately 7 hours
  • Altitude: 11,500 ft to 12,000 ft, via 13,100 ft
  • Trek Gradient: Moderate
  • Water Sources: Carry at least 2 liters of water; refills are available along the way.

Start your day early with a hearty breakfast, as today involves a long trek through verdant meadows and pristine landscapes. Pass through Nichnai Pass, with stunning views of Sonamarg Valley, and culminate at the picturesque Vishnusar Lake.

Day 4: Vishnusar to Gadsar via Kishansar Lake and Gadsar Pass
  • Distance: 14 km
  • Duration: Approximately 10 hours
  • Altitude: 12,000 ft to 12,000 ft, via 13,750 ft
  • Trek Gradient: Moderate
  • Water Sources: Carry at least 2 liters of water; refills are available along the way.

The trek takes you to Kishansar Lake, followed by a descent and flat walk through meadows to Gadsar Pass. Encounter the enchanting Yamsar Lake before reaching Gadsar Lake.

Day 5: Gadsar to Satsar
  • Distance: 9 km
  • Duration: Approximately 6 hours
  • Altitude: 12,000 ft
  • Trek Gradient: Moderate
  • Water Sources: Carry at least 2 liters of water; refills are available along the way.

Begin with a descent from Gadsar Camp, followed by a steep ascent and a gentle walk through meadows to Satsar camp, offering views of seven interconnected lakes.

Day 6: Satsar to Gangabal Twin Lakes
  • Distance: 11 km
  • Duration: Approximately 6 hours
  • Altitude: 12,000 ft to 11,500 ft, via 13,000 ft
  • Trek Gradient: Moderate
  • Water Sources: Carry at least 2 liters of water; refills are available along the way.

Trek towards Gangabal Twin Lakes with an easy ascent, navigating boulder-strewn terrain and forest lines, culminating at the ridge's summit before descending to Nundkol.

Day 7: Gangabal to Naranag and Departure
  • Distance: 15 km
  • Duration: Approximately 7 hours
  • Altitude: 11,500 ft to 7,450 ft
  • Trek Gradient: Moderate
  • Water Sources: Carry at least 2 liters of water; refills are available along the way.

Trek back to Naranag through green meadows and dense pine forests, concluding your journey with a drive back to Srinagar.

Recently Frequently Asked Questions About Kashmir Great Lakes Trek

1. Is the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek suitable for beginners?
The Kashmir Great Lakes Trek is classified as a moderate trek, reaching an altitude of 13,750 feet. Beginners with a reasonable level of fitness can definitely consider this trek. It is advisable to start exercising and walking at least two weeks before the trek to ensure a smoother experience.

2. Where is the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek located?
The trek begins in Sonamarg, which is located in Jammu and Kashmir.

3. What are the physical fitness requirements for the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek?
The Kashmir Great Lakes Trek is considered moderate in difficulty. Anyone who is physically and mentally fit can undertake this trek. It's beneficial to start walking for 30 minutes daily two weeks prior to the trek and gradually increase the intensity to enhance your fitness levels for the trek.

4. When is the best time to do the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek?
The best time to embark on the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek is in July and August.

5. What is the age limit for the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek?
The minimum age requirement for the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek is between 9 to 10 years old.

6. Which mobile networks work on the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek?
The Kashmir Great Lakes Trek is situated at an altitude of 13,750 feet, where mobile networks are typically inaccessible. It’s a good idea to inform your family of this lack of connectivity beforehand.

7. Is there any risk of wild animals or avalanches on the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek?
The risk of avalanches on the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek is minimal, but there is a slight risk of encountering wild animals. It's recommended to stay with your trekking group and not venture off alone.

8. Are liquor and smoking allowed on the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek?
No, liquor and smoking are not allowed in the Himalayas. These activities can negatively affect your health, especially at high altitudes where fresh oxygen is crucial. Smoking and drinking alcohol can exacerbate altitude sickness and other health issues.

9. What is the trek distance of the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek?
The total distance of the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek is approximately 72 kilometers.

10. Is the Kashmir Great Lakes Trek difficult to conquer?
No, the trek is not particularly difficult. Anyone with a decent level of fitness can take on this trek.
